What To Do Before Visiting the Dentist

Knowing what to do in those first few minutes of a dental emergency can make a big difference. If your child’s tooth has been knocked out, gently rinse it with water—avoid touching the root—and, if possible, place it back into the socket or in milk until you reach our office.

For severe pain or swelling, apply a cold compress to the outside of the mouth and call Dental Smart Kids Dentistry immediately. Our caring staff will provide step-by-step instructions to ensure your child’s safety and comfort before you arrive.

We’re always ready to assist with Emergency Pediatric Dentistry in Frisco, offering calm, reliable guidance and immediate care to protect your child’s oral health.

Preventing Pediatric Dental Emergencies

While not every accident can be avoided, there are steps parents can take to minimize dental emergencies. Encourage your child to wear a mouthguard during sports, avoid biting hard objects like ice or pens, and maintain regular dental checkups to detect potential problems early.

Our team at Dental Smart Kids Dentistry focuses on both prevention and education. By building strong daily dental habits, children can keep their teeth healthy and resilient, reducing the risk of injury or decay.
